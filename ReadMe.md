# Customer Data Analysis

An end-to-end data analysis project exploring retail customer shopping behaviour to uncover trends that drive sales, engagement, and long-term loyalty. Raw customer data was cleaned in Python, analysed using SQL, and visualised through an interactive Tableau dashboard.

---

## Business Problem

A leading retail company wants to better understand its customers' shopping behaviour to improve sales, customer satisfaction, and long-term loyalty. Management noticed shifting purchasing patterns across demographics, product categories, and sales channels (online vs offline), and wanted to understand which factors, such as discounts, reviews, seasons, and payment preferences, drive consumer decisions and repeat purchases.

**Core question:** How can the company leverage consumer shopping data to identify trends, improve customer engagement, and optimise marketing and pricing strategies?

---

## Project Workflow

```
Raw Data → Python (cleaning) → SQL (analysis) → Tableau (visualisation)
```

1. **Data Cleaning (Python)** — cleaned and prepared the raw customer dataset, handling missing values, correcting data types, and standardising fields for analysis
2. **Analysis (SQL)** — answered ten business questions using SQL queries covering revenue, segmentation, discounts, and customer behaviour
3. **Visualisation (Tableau)** — built an interactive dashboard to present key findings and enable stakeholders to explore the data

---

## Tech Stack

- **Python** (pandas) — data cleaning and preparation
- **SQL** — business analysis and querying

---

## Business Questions Answered

The analysis addressed ten key questions using SQL:

1. What is the total revenue generated by male vs female customers?
2. Which customers used a discount but still spent more than the average purchase amount?
3. Which are the top 5 products with the highest average review ratings?
4. How does the average purchase amount compare between standard and express shipping?
5. Do subscribed customers spend more? Comparing average spend and total revenue between subscribers and non-subscribers.
6. Which 5 products have the highest percentage of purchases with discounts applied?
7. Segmenting customers into New, Recurring, and Loyal based on their total previous purchases, and showing the count of each segment.
8. What are the top 3 most purchased products within each category?
9. Are repeat buyers (more than 5 previous purchases) also more likely to subscribe?
10. What is the revenue contribution of each age group?

---

## Key Insights


- **Revenue by gender:** Male: $157890, Female: $75191
- **Discount behaviour:** 839 customers used discounts but still spent more than the average purchase amount
- **Customer segments:** 3116 loyal customers, 701 returning customers, 83 new customers
- **Age group contribution:** Young Adults drive the most revenue
